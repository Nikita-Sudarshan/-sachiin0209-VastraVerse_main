Indian Festivals

Immerse yourself in the vibrant celebrations and rich cultural tapestry of India's diverse festivals

Complete Festival Guide

Diwali

Pan-India Autumn

The Festival of Lights, a five-day celebration symbolizing the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

✨ Diwali - The Festival of Lights ✨

Why is Diwali Celebrated?
Diwali celebrates the triumph of good over evil and light over darkness. Across India, it's associated with various legends: Lord Rama’s return to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana, Lord Krishna’s victory over Narakasura, and Goddess Lakshmi’s emergence from the ocean of milk.

How is it Celebrated?
Homes are cleaned and decorated with diyas (oil lamps), candles, and colorful rangolis. People wear new clothes, exchange sweets and gifts, perform Lakshmi Puja, and burst fireworks to celebrate joy and prosperity.

Activities During Diwali:
- Lighting diyas and candles
- Worship of Goddess Lakshmi
- Delicious festive meals and sweets
- Fireworks and crackers (preferably eco-friendly)
- Visiting relatives and community gatherings

Holi

Pan-India Spring

The Festival of Colors, celebrating the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil with vibrant colors.

Navratri

Pan-India Autumn

A nine-night festival celebrating the divine feminine, featuring traditional dances and worship of Goddess Durga.

Durga Puja

East India Autumn

Grand celebration of Goddess Durga's victory over evil, with elaborate pandals and cultural performances.

Pongal

South India Winter

Four-day harvest festival thanking the Sun God, featuring special rice dishes and cattle decoration.

Ganesh Chaturthi

West India Monsoon

Ten-day festival honoring Lord Ganesha, culminating in immersion of idols in water bodies.

Baisakhi

North India Spring

Harvest festival marking Sikh New Year, featuring Bhangra dance, processions, and fairs.

Bihu

Northeast India Spring

Assamese harvest festival with three variations throughout the year, featuring traditional dance and music.

Onam

South India Monsoon

Kerala's harvest festival with boat races, tiger dances, floral decorations, and grand feasts.

Eid ul-Fitr

Pan-India Varies

Festival marking the end of Ramadan with prayers, feasting, gift-giving, and charitable acts.

Christmas

Pan-India Winter

Celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ with midnight mass, carol singing, and festive decorations.

Chhath Puja

East India Autumn

Four-day festival dedicated to Sun God with fasting and offering prayers at riverbanks at sunrise and sunset.

Raksha Bandhan

North India Monsoon

Celebration of brother-sister bond where sisters tie protective threads on brothers' wrists and receive gifts.

Janmashtami

North India Monsoon

Celebration of Lord Krishna's birth with devotional songs, dances, fasting, and Dahi Handi competitions.

Mahashivratri

Pan-India Winter

Worship of Lord Shiva with night-long vigils and prayers, celebrating the divine cosmic energy.

Ram Navami

Pan-India Spring

Celebration of the birth of Lord Ram, the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu, with devotional singing and readings.

Hanuman Jayanti

Pan-India Spring

Celebrating the birth of Lord Hanuman, embodiment of strength and devotion, with prayers and recitation of sacred texts.

Vasant Panchami

North India Spring

Marks the arrival of spring, dedicated to Goddess Saraswati, deity of knowledge, music, and arts.

Karva Chauth

North India Autumn

Married women fast from sunrise to moonrise for their husbands' well-being and long life.

Gudi Padwa

West India Spring

Maharashtrian New Year, celebrated by raising a decorated flag called Gudi and preparing special dishes.

Ugadi

South India Spring

Telugu and Kannada New Year, celebrated with special preparations including Ugadi pachadi, a dish with six flavors.

Akshaya Tritiya

Pan-India Spring

Auspicious day for new beginnings and gold purchases, believed to bring eternal prosperity and good fortune.

Vishu

South India Spring

Malayali New Year, celebrated with Vishukkani (first sight of auspicious items) and giving gifts called Vishukkaineetam.

Govardhan Puja

North India Autumn

Celebrated a day after Diwali, commemorating Lord Krishna lifting Govardhan Hill to protect villagers from rain and floods.

Makar Sankranti

Pan-India Winter

Harvest festival marking the sun's transit into Capricorn with kite flying, special sweets, and ritual baths.
