Women's Traditional Wear

Saree

Pan-Indian

A graceful unstitched drape varying from 5 to 9 yards, wrapped around the waist with one end draped over the shoulder. The garment showcases regional diversity in draping styles, fabric, and patterns.

Regional Variations:

  • Kanjivaram (Tamil Nadu)
  • Banarasi (Uttar Pradesh)
  • Paithani (Maharashtra)
  • Chanderi (Madhya Pradesh)
  • Sambalpuri (Odisha)

Typical Occasions:

Weddings, religious ceremonies, festivals, formal events

Lehenga Choli

North India

A three-piece ensemble consisting of a voluminous ankle-length skirt (lehenga), a fitted blouse (choli), and a dupatta (scarf). Known for intricate embroidery and embellishments.

Types:

  • Rajasthani Lehenga
  • Gujarati Chaniya Choli
  • Bridal Lehenga
  • A-line Lehenga
  • Mermaid Lehenga

Typical Occasions:

Weddings, engagement ceremonies, Garba/Dandiya nights, festive celebrations

Salwar Kameez

North/North-West

A versatile outfit comprising loose trousers (salwar), a long tunic (kameez), and a scarf (dupatta). Available in various styles including Punjabi, Patiala, and Anarkali cuts.

Variations:

  • Patiala Salwar (Punjab)
  • Anarkali Suit
  • Churidar Kameez
  • Palazzo Suit
  • Sharara/Gharara (Muslim influence)

Typical Occasions:

Everyday wear, semi-formal events, college/workplace, festivals

Pavadai Dhavani

South India

Traditional Tamil attire for young girls and women consisting of a long skirt (pavadai), a blouse, and a half-saree (dhavani) draped over the shoulder.

Features:

  • Traditional silk fabric with gold zari work
  • Vibrant colors like maroon, green, and gold
  • Often adorned with traditional motifs
  • Modern versions include Langa Voni and Pattu Pavadai

Typical Occasions:

Coming-of-age ceremonies, temple visits, cultural events, festive celebrations

Mekhela Chador

Northeast India

Traditional Assamese attire consisting of two main pieces: the mekhela (lower garment wrapped around the waist) and the chador (upper garment draped over the body and shoulders).

Features:

  • Usually made of muga silk or pat silk
  • Traditional woven motifs and patterns
  • Geometric designs and natural elements
  • Comes in vibrant colors with golden thread work

Typical Occasions:

Bihu festival, weddings, cultural performances, formal events

Kerala Kasavu

South India

The traditional cream-colored saree with golden border worn by Malayali women in Kerala. Also known as "Mundum Neriyathum" when worn in the traditional two-piece style.

Features:

  • Made of handwoven cotton
  • Distinguished by its golden zari border (kasavu)
  • Simple, elegant design with minimal embellishments
  • Traditional version is off-white with gold border

Typical Occasions:

Onam festival, weddings, temple visits, traditional ceremonies

Ghagra

Rajasthan & Gujarat

A vibrant, flared skirt traditionally worn by women in Rajasthan and Gujarat, often paired with a choli (blouse) and odhani (veil or dupatta).

Features:

  • Full-length, circular skirt with pleats and flare
  • Decorated with mirror work, embroidery, and patchwork
  • Worn with a short choli and a matching odhani
  • Made from cotton, silk, or georgette fabrics

Typical Occasions:

Navratri celebrations, traditional weddings, folk dance performances (like Garba and Ghoomar)

Men's Traditional Wear

Kurta Pajama

North India

A versatile outfit consisting of a long tunic (kurta) paired with loose-fitting pants (pajama). Made in various fabrics from cotton to silk depending on the occasion.

Variations:

  • Straight-cut Kurta
  • Angrakha Kurta (overlapping front)
  • Pathani Suit (from Peshawar region)
  • Lucknowi Chikan Kurta

Typical Occasions:

Daily wear, festivals, religious functions, casual gatherings

Sherwani

North India

An elegant, long coat-like garment that extends below the knees, typically paired with churidar or straight pants. Known for its intricate embroidery and royal appearance.

Types:

  • Classic Sherwani (knee-length)
  • Achkan Sherwani (longer style)
  • Jodhpuri Sherwani (Bandhgala)
  • Indo-Western Sherwani

Typical Occasions:

Weddings, engagement ceremonies, formal events, festive celebrations

Dhoti

Pan-Indian

A rectangular piece of unstitched cloth, usually around 5 yards long, wrapped around the waist and legs in various styles depending on the region. Often paired with a kurta or angavastram.

Regional Styles:

  • Bengali Style (front pleated)
  • Tamil/South Indian Style (Veshti)
  • Marathi Style (Dhotar)
  • Gujarati Style (Panche)

Typical Occasions:

Religious ceremonies, weddings, daily wear (in rural areas), classical performances

Lungi

South/East India

A garment worn around the waist extending to the ankles. Unlike the dhoti, it's sewn into a tube-like shape. Available in various colors and patterns, it's known for comfort in hot climates.

Types:

  • Open Lungi (similar to sarong)
  • Kaili/Mundu (plain white with border)
  • Checkered Pattern (casual wear)
  • Fancy Lungis (festive occasions)

Typical Occasions:

Daily wear, home wear, informal gatherings, relaxed settings

Bandhgala / Jodhpuri

Rajasthan

A structured jacket with a mandarin collar that buttons all the way to the neck. Originally from Jodhpur, it blends Western tailoring with Indian elegance, paired with matching trousers.

Features:

  • Closed mandarin collar
  • Fitted silhouette
  • Multiple buttons down the front
  • Rich fabrics like silk, velvet, or brocade

Typical Occasions:

Black-tie events, diplomatic functions, high-profile weddings, formal gatherings

Mundasa (Turban)

Pan-Indian

Traditional headgear made by wrapping a long piece of cloth around the head. Styles vary greatly across regions, communities, and occasions, representing honor and dignity.

Regional Styles:

  • Rajasthani Safa (colorful, often pink)
  • Punjabi Pagri (Sikh turban)
  • Mysore Peta (South Indian style)
  • Marathi Pheta

Significance:

Symbol of respect, honor, and identity; varies by region, community, and occasion

Traditional Accessories

Bindi

Decorative dot worn on the forehead, traditionally symbolizing the third eye in Hindu tradition. Now also a fashion accessory in various designs.

Bangles (Choodiyan)

Circular bracelet-like ornaments worn by women, made from materials ranging from glass to gold, often worn in sets.

Maang Tikka

Ornament worn on the hairline, typically featuring a pendant that rests on the forehead. Essential in bridal attire across India.

Nath (Nose Ring)

Traditional nose ornament worn by women, ranging from small studs to elaborate rings connected to ear chains.

Traditional Footwear

Juttis

Handcrafted leather footwear with a closed upper and no left-right distinction. Known for colorful embroidery and embellishments, originating from Punjab and Rajasthan.

Kolhapuri Chappals

Handcrafted open sandals made from tanned leather, originating from Kolhapur, Maharashtra. Known for their durability and unique design with toe loop.

Paduka

Ancient toe-knob sandals, considered sacred in Indian culture. Traditionally made of wood, sometimes adorned with silver or ivory for royalty and ceremonial purposes.
